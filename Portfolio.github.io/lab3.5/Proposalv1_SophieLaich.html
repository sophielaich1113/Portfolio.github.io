Hurricane Demographics in North Carolina

Hurricanes in North Carolina affect people, homes, and local economies, hitting some communities harder than others. This project looks at storm severity alongside population and economic data to show which areas are most at risk, and which demographics are most at risk. With increasinly extreme weather patterns due to climate change, these storms are going to cause a significant threat to economically disparaged regions. North Carolina is a state already seeing the consequences of these extreme weather patterns. By geovisualizing the location and significance of these storms, communities and planners can be better prepared to respond, espeically in vulnerable communities.

  1. Describing the purpose of the map. Analyze hurricanes in North Carolina, in their severity and risk to specific populations in the state. This map is intended for state planners and residents to understand regions of most impact and those that need to adopt mitigation and adaptation strategies.
  2. The structure of the map. Include hurricane tracks, severity levels, population demographics, and economic data using choropleth and proportional symbols on a state-scaled map figure. I may also include graphs to show different population demographics or hurricane demographics depending on what I find in analyzing my data.
  3. Data sources. NOAA hurricane data, U.S. Census Bureau demographics of citizens, state economic data.
  4. Methodology. Process datasets and clean and organize them for the information necessary, then integrate into ArcGIS Pro to perform any further actions to create a clear webmap. Design interactive elements for comparisons between figures. If there is extra data that is not necessary, it can be cleaned up and sorted for only the data I need. Once the data is organized and clean, I will convert it to a KML file and create a web map that can be embedded onto the final project website.
